# City Context: Bangalore (Bengaluru)

## Overview

Bangalore, officially known as Bengaluru, is the capital of Karnataka state in South India. Known as the **"Silicon Valley of India"** and **"Garden City,"** it's home to India's largest IT industry and a vibrant cosmopolitan culture blending traditional South Indian heritage with modern urban life.

## Key Facts

- **Population**: ~13 million (metro area)
- **Languages**: Kannada (official), English, Hindi, Tamil, Telugu widely spoken
- **Climate**: Pleasant year-round (15°C - 35°C), monsoon June-September
- **Time Zone**: IST (UTC+5:30)
- **Currency**: Indian Rupee (₹)

## Major Areas & Neighborhoods

### IT Corridors

#### Whitefield
- **Character**: Major IT hub, multinational companies
- **Companies**: Microsoft, SAP, Oracle, Accenture, TCS
- **Pros**: Good infrastructure, malls (Phoenix MarketCity, VR Mall), restaurants
- **Cons**: Traffic nightmare during peak hours, far from city center
- **Best For**: IT professionals, families with kids
- **Rent**: ₹15,000-35,000 (1BHK), ₹25,000-60,000 (2BHK)
- **Commute**: Metro Purple Line, but still traffic-heavy roads

#### Electronic City
- **Character**: Planned IT township, tech parks
- **Companies**: Infosys, Wipro, Biocon, HCL
- **Pros**: Organized layout, good connectivity improving
- **Cons**: Limited nightlife, isolated from main city
- **Best For**: IT professionals, budget-conscious
- **Rent**: ₹12,000-25,000 (1BHK), ₹20,000-45,000 (2BHK)
- **Commute**: NICE Road, upcoming metro connectivity

#### Outer Ring Road (ORR)
- **Character**: Corporate corridor with malls and offices
- **Companies**: Goldman Sachs, Deutsche Bank, Flipkart
- **Areas**: Bellandur, Marathahalli, Kadubeesanahalli
- **Pros**: Modern infrastructure, good restaurants
- **Cons**: Severe traffic, waterlogging during rains
- **Best For**: Corporate professionals
- **Rent**: ₹18,000-40,000 (1BHK), ₹30,000-70,000 (2BHK)

#### Manyata Tech Park
- **Character**: North Bangalore IT hub
- **Companies**: Cognizant, Philips, IBM
- **Pros**: Less traffic than other IT hubs, metro connectivity
- **Cons**: Limited residential options nearby
- **Best For**: North Bangalore residents
- **Rent**: ₹14,000-28,000 (1BHK), ₹22,000-50,000 (2BHK)

### Popular Residential & Social Areas

#### Koramangala
- **Character**: Startup capital, young crowd, vibrant nightlife
- **Blocks**: 1st Block (residential), 5th Block (commercial), 6th Block (pubs)
- **Famous For**: Startups, cafes, restaurants, pubs
- **Pros**: Central location, great food scene, metro connectivity
- **Cons**: Expensive, traffic congestion, parking issues
- **Best For**: Young professionals, entrepreneurs, party lovers
- **Rent**: ₹20,000-45,000 (1BHK), ₹35,000-80,000 (2BHK)
- **Landmarks**: Forum Mall, Sony World Signal, Jyoti Nivas College

#### Indiranagar
- **Character**: Upscale, established, pub culture hub
- **Famous Areas**: 100 Feet Road (pubs), 12th Main (cafes), CMH Road

- **Cons**: Very expensive, limited parking, crowded weekends
- **Best For**: Established professionals, food lovers
- **Rent**: ₹25,000-50,000 (1BHK), ₹40,000-90,000 (2BHK)
- **Landmarks**: Chinnaswamy Stadium, Metro Station, Garuda Mall

#### HSR Layout
- **Character**: IT professionals' haven, planned layout
- **Sectors**: Sector 1 (established), Sector 2 (developing)
- **Pros**: Good food scene, relatively peaceful, planned roads
- **Cons**: Distance from city center, limited metro connectivity
- **Best For**: IT professionals, families
- **Rent**: ₹15,000-35,000 (1BHK), ₹25,000-60,000 (2BHK)
- **Landmarks**: BDA Complex, 27th Main Road restaurants

#### Jayanagar
- **Character**: Traditional, family-oriented, cultural hub
- **Blocks**: 4th Block (shopping), 9th Block (residential)
- **Famous For**: Traditional South Indian food, temples, shopping
- **Pros**: Affordable, good connectivity, cultural activities
- **Cons**: Conservative, limited nightlife
- **Best For**: Families, traditional lifestyle lovers
- **Rent**: ₹12,000-25,000 (1BHK), ₹20,000-45,000 (2BHK)
- **Landmarks**: Ragigudda Temple, Jayanagar Shopping Complex

#### Malleshwaram
- **Character**: Old Bangalore charm, Brahmin community hub
- **Famous Areas**: 8th Cross (food), Sampige Road (shopping)
- **Pros**: Traditional culture, good food, metro connectivity
- **Cons**: Conservative, limited modern amenities
- **Best For**: Traditional families, culture enthusiasts
- **Rent**: ₹14,000-28,000 (1BHK), ₹22,000-50,000 (2BHK)
- **Landmarks**: Kadu Malleshwara Temple, Indian Institute of Science nearby

#### BTM Layout
- **Character**: Budget-friendly, PG hub, young professionals
- **Stages**: 1st Stage (established), 2nd Stage (developing)
- **Pros**: Affordable, good connectivity, lots of PGs
- **Cons**: Crowded, traffic issues, basic infrastructure

- **Landmarks**: Silk Board Junction, BTM Bus Stand

## Local Customs & Culture

### "Adjust Maadi" Culture
Bangalore runs on flexibility. **"Adjust maadi"** (please adjust) is the unofficial motto. Whether it's squeezing into an auto, sharing a table, or dealing with delays - adjustment is expected and appreciated.

### Respect for Elders
- Use **"Sir/Madam"** or **"Anna/Akka"** (brother/sister in Kannada) for strangers
- Touching feet of elders is a sign of respect
- Offer seats to elderly in public transport

### Temple Etiquette
- Remove footwear before entering temples
- Dress modestly (no shorts/sleeveless for men, covered shoulders for women)
- Many temples have specific timings, closed during afternoon

### Weekend Culture
- Bangalore loves its weekends - expect crowded restaurants and pubs
- Sunday brunches are popular
- Many head to nearby hill stations (Nandi Hills, Coorg)

## Weather Patterns

### Summer (March - May)
- Temperature: 25°C - 36°C
- Hot afternoons, pleasant evenings
- Mango season!

### Monsoon (June - September)
- Heavy rains, especially evenings
- Traffic worsens significantly
- Carry umbrella always
- Waterlogging in low-lying areas

### Winter (October - February)
- Temperature: 15°C - 28°C
- Best weather, pleasant throughout
- December-January can get chilly (12°C mornings)

## Local Tips

### Water
- Tap water is **NOT** safe to drink
- Always use bottled or filtered water
- **"Bisleri"** has become generic term for bottled water

### Power Cuts
- Load shedding still happens in some areas
- Keep phone charged
- UPS common in apartments

### Weekday vs Weekend
- **Weekday Bangalore**: Professional, busy, traffic-heavy
- **Weekend Bangalore**: Relaxed, brunch culture, pub hopping

### Bargaining
- Expected in street markets and with auto drivers
- **NOT** done in malls, branded stores, or metered services
- Start at 50% of quoted price in markets

## Safety Tips

- Generally safe city, but stay alert in crowded areas
- Avoid isolated areas late at night
- Keep valuables secure in crowded markets
- Use official taxi apps (Ola, Uber) for safety
- Women: Avoid empty roads late night, use women-only compartments in metro

## Historical Landmarks

### Must-Visit Heritage Sites
- **Bangalore Palace**: Tudor-style architecture, built in 1878, ₹230 entry
- **Tipu Sultan's Summer Palace**: 18th-century Indo-Islamic architecture
- **Vidhana Soudha**: Iconic government building, Neo-Dravidian style
- **Bull Temple (Nandi Temple)**: 16th-century monolithic Nandi statue in Basavanagudi
- **ISKCON Temple**: Modern temple complex, evening aarti is spectacular
- **Bannerghatta National Park**: Safari, butterfly park, 25 km from city center

### Museums
- **Visvesvaraya Industrial & Technological Museum**: Science exhibits, great for kids
- **National Gallery of Modern Art**: Contemporary Indian art
- **HAL Aerospace Museum**: Aviation history, aircraft displays
- **Government Museum**: One of India's oldest, archaeological artifacts

## Accommodation Types

### PG (Paying Guest)
- Popular among students and young professionals
- ₹6,000-15,000/month including food
- Areas: BTM, HSR, Koramangala, Electronic City

### Shared Apartments
- 2-3 people sharing 2BHK/3BHK
- ₹8,000-20,000/person
- Platforms: NoBroker, Flat and Flatmates groups

### Rental Apartments
- 11-month lease standard
- 10-month security deposit typical
- Maintenance charges extra (₹2-5/sq ft)
