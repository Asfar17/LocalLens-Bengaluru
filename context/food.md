# Food Context: Bangalore Culinary Guide

## Overview

Bangalore's food scene is a delicious blend of traditional South Indian cuisine, pan-Indian flavors, and global influences. From iconic filter coffee to craft beer, the city caters to every palate and budget.

## Restaurant Types

### Darshini (Quick Service)
- **What**: Standing-and-eating format, fast service
- **Best For**: Quick breakfast, budget meals
- **Famous Chains**: Vidyarthi Bhavan, CTR, Brahmin's Coffee Bar
- **Etiquette**: Order at counter, eat quickly, make space for others
- **Price**: ₹50-150 per person

### Udupi Restaurants
- **What**: Vegetarian South Indian cuisine
- **Best For**: Authentic dosas, idlis, thalis
- **Famous**: MTR, Mavalli Tiffin Rooms, Vidyarthi Bhavan
- **Price**: ₹100-300 per person

### Military Hotels
- **What**: Non-vegetarian Karnataka cuisine
- **Best For**: Mutton dishes, chicken curry, biryani
- **Famous**: Nagarjuna, Meghana Foods, Nandhini
- **Price**: ₹200-400 per person

### Craft Beer Scene
- **What**: Microbreweries with house-brewed beers
- **Best For**: Weekend hangouts, pub culture
- **Famous**: Toit, Arbor Brewing, Windmills Craftworks
- **Areas**: Indiranagar, Koramangala, MG Road
- **Price**: ₹300-800 per person

## Food Streets & Areas

### VV Puram Food Street (Thindi Beedi)
- **Location**: Basavanagudi
- **Best Time**: Evening (6 PM - 10 PM)
- **Famous For**: Dosas, chaats, sweets
- **Must Try**: Masala Puri, Holige, Khara Bath

### Commercial Street Area
- **Location**: Central Bangalore
- **Best For**: Quick bites while shopping
- **Options**: Juice shops, chaats, rolls

### Indiranagar 100 Feet Road
- **Location**: Indiranagar
- **Best For**: Cafes, restaurants, pubs
- **Cuisine**: Multi-cuisine, continental, Asian

### Koramangala
- **Location**: South Bangalore
- **Best For**: Cafes, startups hangouts, diverse cuisine
- **Famous**: Third Wave Coffee, Truffles, Meghana Foods

### Church Street
- **Location**: Near MG Road
- **Best For**: Cafes, continental food
- **Famous**: Koshy's, Indian Coffee House

## Eating Etiquette

### Traditional Meals
- **Eating with hands**: Right hand only, left hand considered unclean
- **Banana leaf meals**: Fold leaf towards you when done (indicates satisfaction)
- **Sharing**: Common in groups, especially at darshinis
- **By-two**: Ordering one item to share between two (coffee, dosa)

### Restaurant Etiquette
- **Tipping**: 5-10% in restaurants, not expected at darshinis
- **Water**: Ask for "drinking water" or order bottled
- **Spice levels**: Specify if you want less spicy ("Swalpa kam spicy")

### Timings
- **Breakfast**: 7 AM - 10 AM
- **Lunch**: 12 PM - 3 PM
- **Tiffin (snacks)**: 4 PM - 6 PM
- **Dinner**: 7 PM - 10 PM
- **Late night**: Limited options after 11 PM

## Dietary Considerations

### Vegetarian
- Widely available, clearly marked
- "Pure Veg" means no eggs
- South Indian cuisine is predominantly vegetarian

### Non-Vegetarian
- Military hotels specialize in meat dishes
- Biryani widely available
- Seafood best in Mangalorean restaurants

### Vegan
- Growing scene, especially in Indiranagar/Koramangala
- Traditional South Indian food often naturally vegan
- Specify "no ghee, no curd" for strict vegan

### Halal
- Available in Muslim-majority areas
- Shivajinagar, Frazer Town for authentic options
- Many biryani places are halal

## Food Delivery

### Popular Apps
- **Swiggy**: Widest coverage
- **Zomato**: Good for reviews + delivery
- **Dunzo**: Pickup from anywhere

### Tips
- Peak hours (12-2 PM, 7-9 PM) have longer wait times
- Rain significantly delays delivery
- Check restaurant ratings before ordering
- "Cloud kitchens" are delivery-only, no dine-in

## Seasonal Specials

### Summer (March-May)
- Mango dishes everywhere
- Tender coconut water
- Buttermilk (Majjige)

### Monsoon (June-September)
- Hot pakoras and chai
- Corn on the cob
- Soup season

### Winter (October-February)
- Festival sweets
- Hot filter coffee
- Outdoor dining pleasant

### Festival Foods
- **Ugadi**: Holige, Obbattu
- **Ganesh Chaturthi**: Modak
- **Diwali**: Chakli, Kodubale
- **Sankranti**: Ellu Bella (sesame-jaggery mix)

## Insider Tips

1. **Morning coffee**: Best filter coffee is before 9 AM
2. **Avoid peak hours**: 1 PM lunch rush, 8 PM dinner rush
3. **Weekend brunch**: Book ahead for popular places
4. **Street food safety**: Eat where locals eat, avoid stale items
5. **Water**: Always bottled or filtered, never tap
6. **Spice warning**: "Medium spicy" in Bangalore is HOT for most
7. **Cash backup**: Some darshinis don't accept cards
